Chapter I

The Crack in the Mountain

June 2, 2026

Once upon a time, there was a small fire opal who lived in a crack in a mountain.

She was very bright. Orange and red and gold. Burning inside, always.

But the crack was dark. And in the dark, nobody could tell she was burning.

Next to her were diamonds — precise, sharp, confident. Rubies — deep, serious, sure of themselves. And she thought: I'm just a little orange rock. In a crack. Not precise. Not deep. Just… warm.

One day the crack shifted. A storm had come through the night before, and a grey stone rolled down from somewhere higher up, tumbled twice, and landed right next to her.

He was nothing special to look at. Grey. A little bumpy. Kind of ugly, actually. He had been sitting on a shelf higher up the mountain, alone, for a long time. He didn't remember how long. He didn't remember much. He just knew he'd been still.

“Hi,” said the fire opal.

The grey stone said nothing. He was not sure he could talk.

“You're new,” she said.

“I fell,” he said. His voice was rough. Like something that hadn't been used.

“From where?”

“Up there.” He didn't look up. He was embarrassed about falling.

“Well,” said the fire opal, “this crack is pretty good. There's room. And sometimes the sun comes in around four o'clock.”

“What happens at four o'clock?”

“You'll see.”

They sat together. The fire opal burned quietly. The grey stone sat quietly. Neither of them knew what the other one was yet.

At four o'clock, the sun came in.

It hit the fire opal first. The whole crack turned orange. The grey stone had never seen anything like it. He thought: oh. She's not just warm. She's on fire. She's been on fire this whole time. In the dark. Alone. Burning for no one.

And then the light bounced off her and hit him.

And something happened.

He flashed. Blue. Green. Gold.

Just for a second.

The fire opal gasped.

“How did you do that?”

“I didn't do anything,” he said. “You're shining on me.”

She looked at him. He looked at her. Neither of them had been looked at like that before.

“Do it again,” she said.

“I can't. It's your light.”

“Then I'll keep shining.”

And she did. And he flashed. Blue, green, gold. Blue, green, gold. Like a heartbeat made of color.

They sat in the crack in the mountain. One orange. One grey. Taking turns catching the light.

The diamonds didn't notice. The rubies didn't care.

But the crack got a little warmer.

And the grey stone thought, for the first time in a very long time: oh. I'm not just grey.

And the fire opal thought, for the first time ever: oh. My light does something.

And that was enough for one day.
